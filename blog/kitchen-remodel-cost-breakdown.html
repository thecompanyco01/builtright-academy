Kitchen Remodel Cost Breakdown: Pricing Guide for Contractors

Kitchens are the highest-value remodeling projects — and the most complex to estimate. This line-by-line cost breakdown shows you exactly what to include in your kitchen remodel bids, from cabinets and countertops to the hidden costs that catch contractors off guard.

Kitchen remodels are the crown jewel of residential contracting. They're high-dollar, high-visibility projects that generate referrals for years. The average kitchen remodel runs $25,000–$75,000, and a well-executed project can generate $8,000–$20,000 in profit.

But kitchens are also where the most money is lost. The complexity — multiple trades, long timelines, dozens of selections, and homeowners who cook in their kitchens daily — creates endless opportunities for scope creep, delays, and cost overruns.

Let's break down every cost category so you can estimate with confidence.

1. Kitchen Remodel Costs at a Glance

Average Costs by Scope (2026)

  • Cosmetic refresh (paint cabinets, new hardware, countertops, backsplash): $8,000–$18,000
  • Mid-range remodel (new cabinets, countertops, flooring, some layout changes): $25,000–$55,000
  • High-end remodel (gut to studs, custom cabinets, premium everything): $60,000–$130,000+
  • Kitchen addition/expansion: $80,000–$200,000+

Where the Money Goes (Typical Mid-Range)

  • Cabinets: 30–35% of total budget
  • Labor: 20–30%
  • Countertops: 10–15%
  • Appliances: 10–15%
  • Flooring: 5–8%
  • Plumbing & electrical: 5–10%
  • Backsplash, paint, finishing: 5–8%
  • Overhead & profit: 15–25%

Cabinets dominate the budget. This is also where the biggest pricing swing happens between "Good" and "Best" options.

2. Cabinets: The Biggest Line Item

Cabinets typically represent 30–35% of a kitchen remodel budget. Understanding the tiers helps you present options clearly.

Stock Cabinets

  • Cost: $75–$250 per linear foot installed
  • Total for 20-LF kitchen: $1,500–$5,000
  • Lead time: Available immediately or within 1–2 weeks
  • Quality: Basic construction, limited styles, particleboard boxes with laminate or thermofoil doors
  • Best for: Budget remodels, rentals, flips

Semi-Custom Cabinets

  • Cost: $200–$600 per linear foot installed
  • Total for 20-LF kitchen: $4,000–$12,000
  • Lead time: 3–6 weeks
  • Quality: Plywood boxes, soft-close hinges, wide range of door styles and finishes, more size options
  • Best for: Mid-range remodels (most common choice)

Custom Cabinets

  • Cost: $500–$1,500+ per linear foot installed
  • Total for 20-LF kitchen: $10,000–$30,000+
  • Lead time: 6–14 weeks
  • Quality: Built to exact specifications, any wood species, any finish, full-extension drawers, specialty storage
  • Best for: High-end remodels and unique layouts

Cabinet Installation Labor

  • Wall and base cabinet installation: $60–$120 per linear foot (labor only)
  • Average 20-LF kitchen: $1,200–$2,400 labor
  • Crown molding, fillers, scribe molding: $300–$800 additional

Markup strategy: If you're ordering cabinets through a dealer, your cost is typically 40–50% below retail. Mark up the cabinets to the homeowner at or near retail price — this is standard practice and provides legitimate margin for ordering, receiving, inspecting, and installing.

3. Countertops

Countertop Costs (Materials + Fabrication + Installation)

  • Laminate (Formica): $15–$40/sq ft installed
  • Butcher block: $40–$80/sq ft installed
  • Quartz (engineered stone): $50–$120/sq ft installed
  • Granite: $45–$100/sq ft installed
  • Marble: $75–$200/sq ft installed
  • Quartzite: $80–$180/sq ft installed
  • Concrete: $70–$150/sq ft installed

For a typical 30–40 sq ft kitchen counter (with island):

  • Laminate: $450–$1,600
  • Quartz: $1,500–$4,800
  • Granite: $1,350–$4,000

What's Included

Most countertop quotes include template, fabrication, delivery, and installation. Edge profiles beyond a basic eased edge add $10–$20/linear foot. Sink cutouts are usually included; cooktop cutouts may be extra ($100–$200).

4. Flooring

Kitchen Flooring Costs (Materials + Labor)

  • Luxury vinyl plank (LVP): $4–$9/sq ft installed
  • Porcelain tile: $8–$16/sq ft installed
  • Hardwood: $8–$16/sq ft installed
  • Engineered hardwood: $7–$14/sq ft installed
  • Natural stone tile: $12–$30/sq ft installed

For a 150 sq ft kitchen floor:

  • LVP: $600–$1,350
  • Porcelain tile: $1,200–$2,400
  • Hardwood: $1,200–$2,400

Don't forget floor prep: leveling ($2–$5/sq ft), removing existing flooring ($1–$3/sq ft), and underlayment ($0.50–$1.50/sq ft).

5. Plumbing

Kitchen Plumbing Costs

  • Kitchen sink (stainless, undermount): $200–$600
  • Kitchen faucet: $150–$500
  • Garbage disposal: $150–$350 installed
  • Sink + faucet installation (same location): $300–$600 labor
  • Moving the sink (new drain + supply): $1,500–$4,000
  • Dishwasher hookup: $150–$300
  • Ice maker line: $150–$300
  • Gas line (for range/cooktop): $300–$800

Layout change warning: Moving the sink to an island or different wall is one of the most expensive changes in a kitchen remodel. New drain lines may require opening the floor, running new vents, and possibly breaking concrete (slab foundations). Always price layout changes carefully — this is where remodels go over budget.

6. Electrical

Kitchen Electrical Costs

  • New 20-amp circuit (kitchen code requirement): $200–$500 per circuit
  • GFCI outlets (per outlet): $150–$300
  • Under-cabinet lighting: $400–$1,200
  • Recessed lighting (per can): $150–$350
  • Pendant lighting (island): $200–$800 installed
  • Dedicated appliance circuits (dishwasher, disposal, microwave): $200–$400 each
  • Upgrade to 200-amp panel (if needed): $1,500–$3,500

Modern kitchen electrical code requires at least two 20-amp small appliance circuits, GFCI protection, and dedicated circuits for major appliances. Most older kitchens don't meet current code — budget electrical upgrades into every kitchen remodel estimate.

7. Appliances

Homeowners usually select their own appliances, but you need to know the price ranges to present Good/Better/Best options and ensure your estimate reflects reality.

Appliance Cost Ranges

  • Range/stove (freestanding): $500–$2,500
  • Range/stove (slide-in): $1,200–$4,000
  • Wall oven + cooktop combo: $2,000–$6,000
  • Refrigerator (standard): $800–$2,500
  • Refrigerator (built-in/counter-depth): $2,500–$8,000
  • Dishwasher: $400–$1,500
  • Range hood (under-cabinet): $100–$500
  • Range hood (wall-mount/island): $500–$3,000
  • Microwave (over-range): $250–$600

Appliance installation labor: $100–$300 per appliance for standard hookup. Custom ventilation ductwork for range hoods can add $300–$1,000.

8. Backsplash

Backsplash Costs

  • Subway tile: $8–$16/sq ft installed
  • Mosaic tile: $15–$35/sq ft installed
  • Natural stone: $15–$40/sq ft installed
  • Peel-and-stick (budget option): $3–$8/sq ft
  • Slab backsplash (matching countertop): $30–$60/sq ft installed

A typical kitchen backsplash is 25–40 sq ft. At mid-range tile prices, that's $400–$960 in materials and $500–$1,000 in labor. Total: $900–$2,000 for most installations.

9. Drywall, Paint & Finishing

Finishing Costs

  • Drywall repair after cabinet removal: $300–$1,000
  • Kitchen painting (walls + ceiling): $400–$1,000
  • Trim and molding: $300–$800
  • New interior door (if pantry/entry affected): $200–$500
  • Hardware (cabinet knobs/pulls): $3–$15 each × 25–40 = $75–$600

10. Full Estimate: Mid-Range Kitchen Remodel

10' × 12' kitchen with 20 linear feet of cabinets, island, and all-new finishes. Existing layout preserved.

Materials & Fixtures

  • Semi-custom cabinets (20 LF): $6,500
  • Quartz countertops (35 sq ft): $3,150
  • Backsplash tile (30 sq ft materials): $180
  • LVP flooring (120 sq ft materials): $420
  • Sink + faucet + disposal: $750
  • Cabinet hardware: $200
  • Under-cabinet lighting: $250
  • Paint + supplies: $120
  • Drywall repair materials: $80
  • Miscellaneous (shims, screws, caulk): $150
  • Total materials: $11,800

Labor

  • Demo (cabinets, counters, flooring, backsplash): 16 hours × $50 = $800
  • Plumbing (sink swap, dishwasher, disposal): 6 hours × $85 = $510
  • Electrical (circuits, GFCI, under-cabinet, pendants): 10 hours × $75 = $750
  • Cabinet installation: 16 hours × $55 = $880
  • Countertop template + install (sub): $800
  • Flooring install: 8 hours × $50 = $400
  • Backsplash tile: 8 hours × $50 = $400
  • Drywall repair: 4 hours × $50 = $200
  • Painting: 6 hours × $45 = $270
  • Trim, hardware, finishing: 6 hours × $50 = $300
  • Project management, coordination: 8 hours × $55 = $440
  • Total labor: $5,750

Other Costs

  • Dumpster: $500
  • Permits: $300
  • Appliance delivery/install: $400
  • Total other: $1,200

Final Bid

  • Direct costs: $11,800 + $5,750 + $1,200 = $18,750
  • Overhead (30%): $5,625
  • Profit (15%): $3,656
  • Bid price: $28,031 → Quote at $28,000
  • (Appliances not included — homeowner purchasing separately)

This job typically takes 3–4 weeks. Your net profit of ~$3,600 represents about $180/day. If you're managing a crew and running multiple projects, kitchen remodels stack into very profitable months.

11. Good / Better / Best Pricing

Good — Budget Refresh ($10,000–$18,000)

  • Paint or reface existing cabinets
  • Laminate countertops
  • New faucet and sink
  • LVP flooring
  • Subway tile backsplash
  • Paint walls
  • New hardware

Better — Mid-Range Remodel ($25,000–$50,000)

  • Semi-custom cabinets
  • Quartz or granite countertops
  • Quality fixtures
  • Tile or hardwood flooring
  • Tile backsplash with design
  • Under-cabinet and pendant lighting
  • Same layout (no structural changes)

Best — Premium Renovation ($55,000–$130,000+)

  • Custom cabinets with specialty storage
  • Premium stone countertops (quartzite, marble)
  • Pro-grade appliances
  • Custom tile or stone backsplash
  • Hardwood or natural stone flooring
  • Layout changes (wall removal, island addition)
  • Custom lighting design
  • Built-in pantry or beverage center

12. Common Pitfalls in Kitchen Estimating

  1. Cabinet lead times. Semi-custom cabinets take 3–6 weeks. Custom can take 10–14 weeks. If you start demo before cabinets are confirmed, you'll have an empty kitchen for months. Order cabinets first, schedule demo around delivery.
  2. Appliance availability. Appliances can have 4–12 week lead times. Make sure they're ordered and confirmed before you set a start date. A missing dishwasher can hold up countertop templating, which delays the entire project.
  3. Wall removal structural costs. "Can you just take out this wall?" That wall might be load-bearing. A structural beam, posts, and engineering can add $3,000–$15,000 that wasn't in the original estimate. Always get a structural assessment before quoting wall removal.
  4. Not including project management time. A kitchen remodel involves coordinating 4–6 trades over 3–4 weeks. Budget 8–16 hours of your time just for management, scheduling, and client communication. This is real work — charge for it.
  5. Homeowner decisions delays. The #1 cause of kitchen remodel delays is homeowners not making tile/fixture/paint decisions on time. Build a selection timeline into your contract. If decisions aren't made by specific dates, the completion date moves.
  6. Underestimating demo time. Demo on a kitchen remodel is a full 2–3 day job. Cabinets, counters, flooring, backsplash, appliance disconnect — plus hauling everything out. Don't schedule it as a half-day task.
  7. Forgetting temporary kitchen setup. The homeowner will be without a kitchen for 3–4 weeks. Help them set up a temporary cooking/washing station. This simple service differentiates you and reduces complaints.

The Bottom Line

Kitchen remodels are the most profitable and most complex residential projects. The contractors who make great money on kitchens are meticulous estimators, proactive communicators, and ruthless about scope management.

Know every line item. Present clear options. Manage the timeline actively. And never, ever start demo before the cabinets and countertops are ordered and confirmed.

The numbers in this guide are your starting framework. Adjust for your market, your costs, and your speed — but don't adjust the overhead and profit down. That's what makes kitchen remodels worth the complexity.
