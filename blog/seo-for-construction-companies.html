The 3 Pillars of Construction SEO

Pillar 1: Google Business Profile (Local SEO)

For construction companies, Google Business Profile (GBP) is the #1 SEO priority. It's what shows up in the "map pack" when someone searches "general contractor near me" or "kitchen remodeler [city]."

1Claim and verify your listing

Go to business.google.com. If you haven't claimed your listing, do it now. Verification usually takes 3–7 days via postcard.

2Complete every field

  • Business name (exact legal name, no keyword stuffing)
  • Primary category: "General Contractor" or your specific trade
  • Secondary categories: Add all that apply (Remodeler, Home Builder, Kitchen Remodeler, etc.)
  • Service area: Define your exact service radius
  • Hours: Keep accurate (Google penalizes incorrect hours)
  • Services: List every service with descriptions
  • Products: Add your main service packages with photos

3Photos — your secret weapon

Upload 20+ high-quality photos: completed projects (before/after), your team, vehicles, office. Add 2–3 new photos weekly. Google prioritizes listings with recent, relevant photos.

4Reviews — the ranking factor you control

Reviews are the #1 ranking factor for the map pack. Here's the system:

  • Ask every customer at project completion (in person, then follow up via text with a direct link)
  • Respond to EVERY review within 24 hours (positive and negative)
  • Never offer incentives for reviews (violates Google's policy)
  • Target: 5+ reviews/month until you hit 50+, then maintain 2–3/month
💡 Review hack: Create a short link (bit.ly or similar) that goes directly to your Google review page. Print it on your business card, invoice, and as a QR code on a "Thank You" sign you put up at every job site.

Pillar 2: Website SEO (Organic Rankings)

Your website is where organic search traffic converts to leads. Here's how to optimize it:

Homepage

  • Title tag: "[Company Name] — [Service] in [City, State]"
  • H1 heading: Focus on your primary service + location
  • Above the fold: Phone number, contact form, and 1 clear call to action
  • Social proof: Reviews, certifications, years in business
  • Service areas listed with internal links

Service Pages (Critical for Rankings)

Create a dedicated page for every service you offer:

  • /kitchen-remodeling
  • /bathroom-remodeling
  • /deck-building
  • /home-additions
  • /basement-finishing

Each page should include: service description, process, pricing range, FAQs, photos, and a contact form. Don't put all services on one page — Google rewards depth.

Location Pages (Programmatic SEO Opportunity)

If you serve multiple cities, create a page for each:

  • /kitchen-remodeling-austin-tx
  • /kitchen-remodeling-round-rock-tx
  • /kitchen-remodeling-cedar-park-tx

Each page needs unique content — not just the city name swapped. Include local landmarks, local building codes/permits, and project photos from that area. This is one of the highest-ROI SEO tactics for contractors with a 30+ mile service area.

Technical SEO Checklist for Contractors

You don't need to be a developer, but these basics must be right:

  1. Mobile-friendly: 70%+ of "contractor near me" searches happen on phones. Test your site at Google's Mobile-Friendly Test.
  2. Fast loading: Under 3 seconds. Compress images (TinyPNG), use a CDN, and choose fast hosting.
  3. SSL certificate: Your site must be HTTPS. No exceptions. Chrome literally warns users about non-HTTPS sites.
  4. XML sitemap: Submit to Google Search Console. Update when you add new pages.
  5. Schema markup: Add LocalBusiness schema to your homepage. Adds review stars, location, and hours to search results.
  6. Meta titles & descriptions: Unique for every page. Include your target keyword and location.
  7. Internal linking: Every page should link to 3–5 related pages on your site.

Link Building for Construction Companies

Backlinks (other websites linking to yours) are a major ranking factor. Here are tactics that work for contractors:

  • Supplier directories: Your material suppliers (Andersen Windows, James Hardie, etc.) often have contractor locators. Get listed.
  • Local business directories: Chamber of Commerce, BBB, local business associations
  • Houzz profile: Upload projects, get reviews. Houzz profiles rank well and link back to your site.
  • Industry associations: NARI, NAHB, local HBA chapters — membership often includes a directory listing
  • Local sponsorships: Sponsor a little league team, charity event, or community project. Get a link from their website.
  • Guest posts: Write for local real estate blogs, home improvement sites, or your city's online magazine

FAQ

How long does SEO take to work for a construction company?

Expect to see meaningful results in 3–6 months. Google Business Profile improvements can show results faster (2–4 weeks). Blog content typically takes 3–6 months to rank. The good news: once you rank, results compound — you keep getting leads without ongoing ad spend.

Is SEO worth it for a small construction company?

Absolutely. In fact, small companies benefit MORE from SEO because it levels the playing field. A well-optimized GBP listing and a handful of great reviews can outrank large companies with bigger ad budgets.

What keywords should construction companies target?

Start with "[your service] [your city]" keywords: "kitchen remodeler Austin TX", "general contractor Dallas". Then expand to cost-related queries: "kitchen remodel cost Austin" and comparison queries: "best general contractor in [city]".

Should I hire an SEO company?

Not until you understand the basics. Too many contractors waste $2,000/month on agencies that deliver nothing because they can't evaluate the work. Learn the basics (this guide is a great start), try it yourself, and only hire an agency when you've outgrown DIY.
