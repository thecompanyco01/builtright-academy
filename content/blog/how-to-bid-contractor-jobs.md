## Outline

### Introduction
- The #1 mistake tradespeople make: pricing based on gut feel instead of real numbers
- Why most contractors leave 15-30% on the table on every job
- This guide is for the solo electrician or small plumbing shop, NOT mega-contractors

### 1. Know Your Real Costs (Most Don't)
- Direct costs: materials, labor (including YOUR time), equipment
- Indirect costs: truck, insurance, tools, phone, licensing
- Overhead: office, bookkeeping, marketing, callbacks/warranty work
- **The hourly rate trap:** Why "$75/hour" doesn't mean you're making $75/hour
- Exercise: Calculate your true fully-loaded hourly cost

### 2. The Bidding Framework: Cost + Markup + Profit
- Cost: What the job actually costs you
- Markup: Covers overhead and risk (typically 20-50% for residential trades)
- Profit: What you actually take home (target 10-20% net)
- Why "I'll match any price" is a race to bankruptcy

### 3. How to Estimate a Job in 5 Steps
1. Site visit — never bid blind
2. Material takeoff — itemize everything
3. Labor hours — be honest, add 15% buffer
4. Factor in complexity/risk (old homes, permits, access issues)
5. Apply your markup and profit margin

### 4. Pricing Strategies That Win Jobs AND Make Money
- Good/Better/Best pricing (3 tiers always)
- Flat rate vs. time & materials — when to use each
- The "anchor" technique: lead with the premium option
- Seasonal pricing: raise rates during peak season

### 5. Common Bidding Mistakes That Kill Profits
- Not accounting for drive time and setup
- Forgetting permit fees and inspection costs
- Underbidding to "stay busy" (busy and broke)
- Not updating prices for material cost increases
- Giving quotes over the phone without seeing the job

### 6. How to Present Your Bid and Close the Deal
- Written proposals beat verbal quotes (always)
- Walk them through the bid — explain the value
- Handle the "your price is too high" objection
- When to walk away from a job
