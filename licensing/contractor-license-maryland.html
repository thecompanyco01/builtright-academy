📝 How to Get a Contractor License in Maryland

1

Meet Basic Eligibility

You must be at least 18 years old, have relevant experience in construction (typically 2-4 years), and have a clean criminal background. Some states also require proof of financial solvency.

2

Complete Registration

Complete the registration application with the Maryland Home Improvement Commission. No exam is required.

3

Get Insurance & Coverage

Obtain general liability insurance (minimum $500,000-$1,000,000 recommended). Workers' compensation insurance is also required if you have employees.

4

Submit Your Application

File your application with the Maryland Home Improvement Commission. Include all required documentation, proof of insurance, and application fee ($300-$500).

5

Maintain Your License

Renew your license biennial. Keep your insurance and current at all times.

💰 Cost of Getting Licensed in Maryland

The total cost to obtain a contractor license in Maryland includes:

  • Application fee: $300-$500
  • General liability insurance: $500-$2,000/year (varies by coverage)
  • Workers' compensation: Required if you have employees ($1,000-$5,000+/year)
  • Business registration: $50-$500 depending on entity type

Estimated total startup cost: $1,500-$5,000 depending on your license class and business structure.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a contractor license in Maryland?

Yes. Maryland requires a contractor license for projects valued at $500 or more. The license is issued by the Maryland Home Improvement Commission.

How long does it take to get a contractor license in Maryland?

The process typically takes 2-8 weeks, depending on application volume, and completeness of your documentation. Having all your paperwork ready can speed up the process significantly.

Can I work in Maryland with an out-of-state license?

Generally no. Maryland requires its own state-specific license. You should contact the Maryland Home Improvement Commission for the most current reciprocity information.
