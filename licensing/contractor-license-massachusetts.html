📝 How to Get a Contractor License in Massachusetts

1

Meet Basic Eligibility

You must be at least 18 years old, have relevant experience in construction (typically 2-4 years), and have a clean criminal background. Some states also require proof of financial solvency.

2

Pass the Required Exams

The Massachusetts contractor exam typically covers trade knowledge, business law, safety regulations, and project management. Study guides and prep courses are available.

3

Get Insurance & Coverage

Obtain general liability insurance (minimum $500,000-$1,000,000 recommended). Workers' compensation insurance is also required if you have employees.

4

Submit Your Application

File your application with the Office of Consumer Affairs and Business Regulation. Include all required documentation, proof of insurance, and application fee ($200-$350).

5

Maintain Your License

Renew your license biennial and complete 12 hours of continuing education. Keep your insurance and current at all times.

💰 Cost of Getting Licensed in Massachusetts

The total cost to obtain a contractor license in Massachusetts includes:

  • Application fee: $200-$350
  • Exam fee: $75-$150 (if required)
  • General liability insurance: $500-$2,000/year (varies by coverage)
  • Workers' compensation: Required if you have employees ($1,000-$5,000+/year)
  • Business registration: $50-$500 depending on entity type

Estimated total startup cost: $1,500-$5,000 depending on your license class and business structure.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a contractor license in Massachusetts?

Yes. Massachusetts requires a contractor license for projects valued at $0 or more. The license is issued by the Office of Consumer Affairs and Business Regulation.

How long does it take to get a contractor license in Massachusetts?

The process typically takes 2-8 weeks, depending on application volume, exam scheduling, and completeness of your documentation. Having all your paperwork ready can speed up the process significantly.

Can I work in Massachusetts with an out-of-state license?

Generally no. Massachusetts requires its own state-specific license. You should contact the Office of Consumer Affairs and Business Regulation for the most current reciprocity information.
