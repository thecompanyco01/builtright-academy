📝 How to Get a Contractor License in Tennessee

1

Meet Basic Eligibility

You must be at least 18 years old, have relevant experience in construction (typically 2-4 years), and have a clean criminal background. Some states also require proof of financial solvency.

2

Pass the Required Exams

The Tennessee contractor exam typically covers trade knowledge, business law, safety regulations, and project management. Study guides and prep courses are available.

3

Get Insurance & Bonding

Obtain general liability insurance (minimum $500,000-$1,000,000 recommended) and a surety bond of $10,000. Workers' compensation insurance is also required if you have employees.

4

Submit Your Application

File your application with the Tennessee Board for Licensing Contractors. Include all required documentation, proof of insurance, bond certificate, and application fee ($250-$500).

5

Maintain Your License

Renew your license annual. Keep your insurance and bond current at all times.

💰 Cost of Getting Licensed in Tennessee

The total cost to obtain a contractor license in Tennessee includes:

  • Application fee: $250-$500
  • Exam fee: $75-$150 (if required)
  • General liability insurance: $500-$2,000/year (varies by coverage)
  • Surety bond: $10,000 (premium is typically 1-15% of bond amount)
  • Workers' compensation: Required if you have employees ($1,000-$5,000+/year)
  • Business registration: $50-$500 depending on entity type

Estimated total startup cost: $1,500-$5,000 depending on your license class and business structure.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a contractor license in Tennessee?

Yes. Tennessee requires a contractor license for projects valued at $25,000 or more. The license is issued by the Tennessee Board for Licensing Contractors.

How long does it take to get a contractor license in Tennessee?

The process typically takes 2-8 weeks, depending on application volume, exam scheduling, and completeness of your documentation. Having all your paperwork ready can speed up the process significantly.

Can I work in Tennessee with an out-of-state license?

Generally no. Tennessee requires its own state-specific license. You should contact the Tennessee Board for Licensing Contractors for the most current reciprocity information.
