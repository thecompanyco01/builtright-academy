How to Calculate Board Feet

A board foot is the standard unit of measurement for hardwood lumber in the United States. One board foot equals 144 cubic inches of wood — equivalent to a board that is 1 inch thick, 12 inches wide, and 12 inches long. Understanding board feet is essential for buying hardwood lumber, estimating project costs, and comparing prices between suppliers.

Board Foot Formula

The formula to calculate board feet is straightforward:

BF = (T × W × L) ÷ 144
All dimensions in inches

Or, if length is in feet:

BF = (T × W × L) ÷ 12
Thickness & width in inches, length in feet

Tips for Buying Hardwood Lumber

  • Buy rough-sawn to save money. Surfaced lumber (S2S or S4S) costs 10–20% more because the mill has already planed it smooth. If you have a planer, buy rough and save.
  • Buy wider boards. Narrow boards (under 4") cost more per board foot because they require more handling. Boards 6"+ wide are usually the best value.
  • Add 15–25% for waste. Defects, knots, and cutting waste mean you'll use less than you buy. For complex projects, add 25%. For simple cuts, 15% is usually enough.
  • Check moisture content. Kiln-dried lumber (6–8% MC) is ready to use. Air-dried lumber (12–15% MC) may need further drying before fine woodworking.
  • Ask about FAS grade for the best clear boards. #1 Common is significantly cheaper and perfect for projects where you can cut around defects.
