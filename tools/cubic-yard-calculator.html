How to Calculate Cubic Yards

A cubic yard is a unit of volume equal to 27 cubic feet (3 ft × 3 ft × 3 ft). It's the standard measurement for ordering bulk materials like concrete, gravel, sand, topsoil, and mulch. Getting the right quantity saves money and prevents project delays from short orders or wasted material.

Cubic Yard Formula

For rectangular areas (the most common):

Cubic Yards = (Length × Width × Depth) ÷ 27

All dimensions must be in feet. If depth is in inches, divide by 12 first. For example: a 20 ft × 10 ft patio with 4 inches of gravel = (20 × 10 × 0.333) ÷ 27 = 2.47 cubic yards.

Ordering Tips

  • Always order 5–10% extra. Uneven ground, compaction, and waste mean you'll use more than calculated. Running short mid-project is worse than having a little extra.
  • Ask about minimum delivery. Most suppliers require a minimum order (often 2–3 yards) or charge a small-load fee for under-minimum orders.
  • Wet materials weigh more. Rain-soaked topsoil or wet sand can weigh 20–30% more, which matters for truck weight limits and structural calculations.
  • Compaction factor: Loose gravel and soil compact 10–15% when tamped. Add that to your order quantity.
  • Delivery access: Ensure a dump truck can reach your site. Standard trucks need 10 ft width and 14 ft overhead clearance.
