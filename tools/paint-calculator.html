How to Estimate Paint: Contractor's Guide

The Basic Formula

Gallons = (Paintable Area × Coats) ÷ Coverage Rate

Paintable area = total wall area minus doors and windows. Standard coverage is 350 sq ft per gallon for smooth walls. Textured surfaces use 250–300 sq ft per gallon.

Pricing Paint Jobs as a Contractor

Most painting contractors charge $2–$6 per square foot for interior painting, including labor and materials. A typical 12×12 room with 8-foot ceilings costs $800–$2,000 depending on complexity, prep work, and finish quality.

  • Labor: 60–70% of total job cost
  • Materials: 15–25% (paint, primer, tape, drop cloths)
  • Overhead & profit: 15–25%

Use a minimum 50% markup on materials and charge labor at $35–$65/hour per painter depending on your market.

When to Use Primer

  • Painting over bare drywall or patches (always prime)
  • Going from dark to light colors
  • Covering stains or smoke damage (use shellac-based primer)
  • Painting over glossy surfaces (scuff sand + prime)
  • New wood trim or cabinets
