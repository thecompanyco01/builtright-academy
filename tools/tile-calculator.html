How to Calculate Tile for Any Project

Getting the right amount of tile is crucial — running short mid-project means a trip back to the store and risking a different dye lot. Buying too much wastes money. This guide covers the exact math for floors, walls, backsplashes, and showers.

The Basic Formula

Area (sq ft) = Length × Width

Then: Number of Tiles = Area ÷ Tile Size (in sq ft) + waste percentage

For grout lines, add the grout width to each tile dimension before calculating coverage. A 12×12 tile with 1/8" grout lines effectively covers 12.125 × 12.125 inches — which adds up over large areas.

Materials Checklist Beyond Tile

Don't forget these essential supplies when estimating your project budget:

  • Thinset morite: 1 bag (50 lb) covers ~60–80 sq ft. Budget $15–$30/bag.
  • Grout: 1 bag (25 lb) covers ~75–200 sq ft depending on tile size and joint width. $12–$25/bag.
  • Backer board: Required over plywood subfloors. $10–$15 per 3×5 sheet. You need one sheet per 15 sq ft.
  • Tile spacers: $3–$8 per bag. Get the size matching your grout line width.
  • Sealer: For natural stone and unglazed porcelain. $15–$30 per quart.
  • Transition strips: Where tile meets other flooring. $5–$20 each.

Pro Tips for Accurate Tile Estimation

  • Measure each wall or section separately — especially for backsplashes with windows, outlets, and varied heights.
  • Subtract areas you won't tile: vanity footprint, toilet flange, window openings. But keep the waste factor — it already accounts for this.
  • Always round up to full boxes. You can't buy partial boxes, and leftover tiles are cheap insurance for future repairs.
  • Check the dye lot. Tiles from different manufacturing batches may have slight color differences. Buy everything at once.
  • Large-format tiles cost more to install because they require a flat substrate, more thinset, and careful handling.
