How to Determine Wire Size for Electrical Circuits

Choosing the correct wire size is critical for safety and performance. Undersized wire overheats, creating a fire hazard. Oversized wire wastes money. The right wire gauge depends on four factors: amperage (current draw), distance (wire run length), voltage, and acceptable voltage drop. This guide explains how electricians size wire for residential and commercial circuits.

Wire Sizing Tips

  • Continuous loads require 125% sizing. If a load runs for 3+ hours (water heater, AC, EV charger), the NEC requires the circuit to be sized at 125% of the load. A 30A continuous load needs a 40A circuit.
  • Voltage drop matters more at 120V. The same wire at 120V has twice the percentage voltage drop compared to 240V. Long 120V runs often need upsized wire.
  • Use THHN/THWN in conduit. NM-B (Romex) can only be used in dry locations inside framing. For conduit runs, garages, and exposed wiring, use individual THHN or THWN conductors.
  • Derate for bundling. More than 3 current-carrying conductors in conduit require ampacity derating per NEC Table 310.15(C)(1). This can reduce ampacity by 20–50%.
  • Ground wire can be smaller. Equipment grounding conductors follow NEC Table 250.122 and are typically 1–2 sizes smaller than the circuit conductors.
